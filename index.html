Saif Oreibi was born in Baghdad, Iraq on 3rd June 1986, and was raised in a small family. He studied business for two years, and then he was kidnapped due to the insecure conditions of the country, next, they moved to Amman then Syria, and then settled in Egypt in 2006. His career began when he started to learn the Saz instrumental music at the house of Oud with Nasir Shamma, then he produced a ( Gilgamesh album ) containing 13 pieces of music in 2013. Later on, he established a studio (One Take) in 2014 and produced many of the musical compositions and poems with Muhammad Hassan, Imad Kamal, Asaad Al-Ghariri, and the Iskandarella band, it was a brilliant success. Then he was offered to compose the music for the fourth pyramid movie, so he read the script and admired it, so he decided to be the producer of the film, so he enrolled in the world of production and produced The Monkey Talks, then 122, and then many artworks. The fourth pyramid movie is his favorite artwork, as it is an integrated work of producing, corrupting, distributing, and playing. During his artistic career, he faced many challenges; first, he is an Iraqi producer who produces Egyptian films. In addition, Media rumors about him and his country. “Working with the media is not easy, but it requires discipline, respect, and responsibility for all risks.”
